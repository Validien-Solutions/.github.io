Machine Learning

Machine Learning is a sub-category in the topic of Data Science. Data science describes the statistical modeling and detection of clusters, the multi-variate methods of discriminant analysis and predictive modeling. Machine Learning encompasses the second two.

Predictive analytics is the set-modeling of a formula to conform to the "best fit" for independent and dependent variables within a training sample of your data. A second set of data is kept to validate the model. The real trick in the practice of Machine Learning is becoming very familiar with your source data, and "munging" (transforming it as an intermediate step) to provide guidance to the model in how to interpret categories, progression in a direction, or simply to reduce autocorrelated members from re-inforcing themselves. With proper preparation and discriminant analysis, patterns can be teased out of the data if they have any correlation at all. Credit scoring, pricing models, and and costs can be predicted in this way, assuming the relevant inputs are, in face, relevant to an outcome.

Some of the training data sets on Kaggle are an excellent place to start in Data Science. Validien has experience in SAS, R-Studio, and Azure Synapse supporting Machine Learning.
